# LastZ Alliance Staff Structure (S1–S4)

*Organizational framework for alliance leadership, aligned to US Army staff functions and LastZ game mechanics.*

**NOTE:** 
This is ideation for consideration. The US Army uses this structure successfully, and as this is a war-oriented game, I thought it would be insightful and helpful to map proven military organization to the game mechanics/structure. 

The game should be fun. Some people may not see formality as fun. That's ok. This is only a reference for consideration. 
---

## Overview

This structure assigns clear roles and responsibilities so the alliance can plan, execute, and sustain operations—especially during Cross-State Capital Clash, Alliance Duel, and other key events. Each staff section has a defined mission and deliverables.

| Section | Army Function | LastZ Alliance Role | Primary Output |
|--------|----------------|---------------------|----------------|
| **S1** | Personnel | Members & Soldiers | Roster, readiness, discipline |
| **S2** | Intelligence | Intelligence | Threat picture, targeting, intel reports |
| **S3** | Operations / Training | Operations & Training | Plans, rallies, event execution, member capability |
| **S4** | Logistics / Supply | Supplies & Resources | RSS, event prep, sustainability |

---

## S1 — Personnel / Members / Soldiers

**Mission:** Maintain alliance roster, readiness, and order. Ensure the right people are in the right roles and that membership supports the alliance’s goals.

### Responsibilities

- **Roster & activity**
  - Track membership (join/leave, rank, title).
  - Monitor activity (logins, participation in rallies/events).
  - Flag inactive or at-risk members for S1/S3 discussion.
- **Recruitment & retention**
  - Define recruitment standards (power, activity, language, time zone).
  - Run recruitment channels and vet applicants.
  - Onboard new members (rules, Discord/chat, S1–S4 points of contact).
- **Discipline & rules**
  - Enforce alliance rules (e.g., farming limits, 6-attack rule, zombie siege behavior).
  - Issue warnings and escalate to leadership when needed.
  - Maintain a short, clear “rules doc” (link to alliance rules / S4 farming policy).
- **Readiness reporting**
  - Simple weekly/monthly readiness snapshot: active count, power distribution, key contributors.
  - Feed S3 (who can rally, who needs training) and S2 (who can scout).

### LastZ Game Alignment

- Alliance member list, titles, and ranks.
- Participation in Alliance Duel, Capital Clash, and daily activities.
- Compliance with alliance terms (e.g., [LastZ Alliance Terms and Rules](https://lastz.online/rules/) style policies).

### Recommendations

1. **Define “active” clearly** (e.g., login X days/week, or participation in Y events per month) so S1 reports are consistent.
2. **Keep a deputy S1** so roster and discipline are covered during busy events or leader absence.
3. **Cross-talk with S3:** S1 identifies who is active; S3 uses that for rally fills and training focus.

---

## S2 — Intelligence

**Mission:** Build and maintain the intelligence picture. Support decision-making with accurate, timely information on enemy states, matchups, and threats.

### Responsibilities

- **Enemy state assessment**
  - Track state scores, rankings, and historical performance (Capital Clash, Alliance Duel).
  - Identify strong/weak alliances and key players (power, activity patterns).
- **Capital Clash intel**
  - Monitor matchup: who is Defender vs Invader; state scores; which alliances hold turrets/capital.
  - Track progress (%), turret ownership, and durability implications (e.g., 2x durability loss in capital zone).
  - Identify high-value targets (trucks, HQs in capital zone) and relay to S3.
- **Scouting & targeting**
  - Coordinate scouts for enemy state layout, turret/capital status, and resource nodes.
  - Maintain simple target lists (by priority) for rallies and solo hits.
  - Support “enemy buster” and plunder objectives (e.g., “Successfully plunder an enemy State truck”).
- **Intel products**
  - **Pre–Capital Clash:** State matchup brief (scores, likely defender/invader, key alliances).
  - **During event:** Situation updates (turrets, capital %, key threats).
  - **Post-event:** After-action summary (what worked, enemy tactics, lessons for S3).

### LastZ Game Alignment

- State Score and matchmaking (who we face in Cross-State Capital Clash).
- Turret and Capital capture status; 100% progress and win conditions.
- Invader vs Defender roles (Friday “Apocalypse Time”).
- Event objectives that require intel: plunder enemy truck, Arena, Full Preparedness, Alliance Duel rankings.

### Recommendations

1. **Standardize reports:** Use a simple template (State, Score, Top alliances, Turrets/Capital status, Recommended actions) so S3 and leadership can act quickly.
2. **Assign S2 scouts:** Designate members (and backups) for scouting; S2 tasks them and consolidates results.
3. **Time intel to decisions:** Pre-weekend brief by Friday; updates at set times during Saturday (e.g., 12:00, 14:00, 16:00) so S3 can adjust plans.
4. **OPSEC:** Keep sensitive targeting and timing in leadership/S3 channels; avoid broadcasting exact rally times in open chat.

---

## S3 — Operations & Training

**Mission:** Plan and execute operations; train members so the alliance can fight effectively in Capital Clash, Alliance Duel, and other events.

### Responsibilities

- **Operations**
  - Draft operation plans for Capital Clash (turrets, capital, rally sequence).
  - Coordinate rallies: who leads, who fills, timing, and objectives (turrets vs capital vs cleanup).
  - Synchronize with S2 (targets) and S4 (resource and speed-up availability).
  - Run Alliance Duel planning: daily objectives, matchups, rally focus.
- **Training**
  - Teach core mechanics: research trees (military strategies, army building), troop types, and rally roles.
  - Run “how to” briefs or docs: joining rallies, shielding, when to attack vs defend.
  - Identify skill gaps (from S1 activity + S3 observation) and prioritize training.
- **Event execution**
  - Full Preparedness: what tasks to do, in what order, to avoid overspending (per [LastZ Capital Clash guidance](https://lastz.online/svs/)).
  - Alliance Duel: daily rank goals, rally schedule.
  - Capital Clash: timeline (Saturday 12:00–16:00 capital contest, 24:00 end), turret limits (10 troops each), capital limit (10 troops).
- **Doctrine & tactics**
  - Document preferred tactics (e.g., turret capture order, capital push timing).
  - After-action reviews with S2; update tactics and training based on results.

### LastZ Game Alignment

- Cross-State Capital Clash: contest phase, turret mechanics (auto-attacks on enemy capital), victory conditions.
- Alliance Duel: daily missions, rankings, victory conditions.
- Research priorities: which military and army-building nodes to push (Fire Up, Armor, Joint Operation, etc.) for the current meta.
- Rally mechanics: who leads, fill requirements, and event-specific rally rules.

### Recommendations

1. **Publish an event calendar:** Key times (Saturday 12:00, 16:00, 24:00; Alliance Duel resets) so S1/S2/S4 can align.
2. **Rally roster:** S3 maintains “rally leads” and “reliable fillers” (from S1 data); S3 assigns leads and backup leads per event.
3. **One-page battle drill:** Short checklist for Capital Clash (e.g., “Turrets first → then capital”; “Check S2 target list before rally”).
4. **Train to the event:** Before each Capital Clash, run one short training session or recap on turret/capital rules and current plan.

## S4 — Supplies & Resources

**Mission:** Ensure the alliance has the resources and sustainability to support operations and event objectives without wasteful spending.

### Responsibilities

- **Resource management**
  - Advise on RSS gathering: tiles, farming zones, and alliance resource rules.
  - Monitor farming limits (e.g., 6 attacks per player) and help avoid violations.
  - Coordinate with S3 so members don’t overspend RSS needed for Full Preparedness or Alliance Duel.
- **Event preparedness**
  - Full Preparedness: track task requirements and advise what to save (RSS, speedups, items).
  - Capital Clash: remind members to preserve resources for weekend clash and Alliance Duel.
  - Publish “what to save” and “what you can spend” windows.
- **Sustainability**
  - Healing and speed-up usage: when to use for events vs save.
  - Truck defense: tips to reduce plunder loss (per S2 threat picture).
  - Long-term resource planning (e.g., research costs, upgrade queues).
- **Alliance support**
  - Donations, help requests, and alliance tech if applicable.
  - Any in-game “supply” or reinforcement mechanics (e.g., rss aid, reinforcement rules).

### LastZ Game Alignment

- Farming and alliance rules (e.g., 6-attack limit, zombie siege).
- Full Preparedness tasks and resource demands.
- Alliance Duel daily missions that consume RSS or items.
- Capital Clash: “don’t overspend resources you’ll need” (official guidance).
- Diamond mines (invader victory reward): S4 can advise on prioritizing and protecting them post-win.

### Recommendations

1. **Pre-event supply memo:** Before each major event, S4 publishes “Save list” and “OK to use” so members don’t break Full Preparedness or clash readiness.
2. **Farming policy doc:** One place that states farming limits, zombie siege rules, and consequences; S1 enforces, S4 explains.
3. **S4–S3 coordination:** S4 flags “resource crunch” periods; S3 avoids scheduling heavy rallies or spending spikes during those windows when possible.

---

## Coordination & Command

- **Leadership (President / senior leadership):** Sets strategy, approves major plans, resolves conflicts between S1–S4, and represents the alliance in state-level coordination (e.g., Capital Clash state unity).
- **S1–S4 sync:** Regular short syncs (e.g., weekly; daily during Capital Clash) to align roster (S1), intel (S2), plans (S3), and supply (S4).
- **S2–S3 link:** S2 produces intel and target lists; S3 uses them for rally plans and battle drills. Strong S2–S3 communication is critical for Capital Clash.
- **S3–S4 link:** S3 plans event tempo; S4 advises on sustainability and what members should save or spend.

## Next Steps for Discussion

1. **Assign incumbents:** Who fills S1, S2, S3, S4 (and deputies)?
2. **Channels:** Separate channels or threads for S1 (roster), S2 (intel), S3 (ops/training), S4 (supply)?
3. **Templates:** S2 intel template; S3 one-page battle drill; S4 pre-event save list.
4. **Cadence:** Weekly staff sync; event-specific syncs (e.g., Friday and Saturday for Capital Clash).
5. **Review:** After each Capital Clash, short AAR with S2 (what intel helped) and S3 (what to change); S4 and S1 add sustainability and roster lessons.

This structure is a starting point. Adjust titles and responsibilities to fit your alliance size, activity level, and culture while keeping the S1–S4 logic so you can scale and refine strategies over time.
