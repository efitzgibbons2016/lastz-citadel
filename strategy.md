# LastZ Alliance Strategy

*Strategic guidance for Growth & Preparedness, Offensive Posture, and Defensive Posture. Aligned to the alliance org structure (S1–S4) and LastZ game systems.*

**NOTE:** 
This strategy is ideation for consideration. It assumes the use of S1-S4 org structure. We can update this to use our R4 titles/roles. 

The game should be fun. Some people may not see formality as fun. That's ok. This is only a reference for consideration. 

---

## Strategic Framework

Three pillars support sustained alliance effectiveness:

| Pillar | Objective | Primary Staff | Key Game Systems |
|--------|-----------|---------------|------------------|
| **Growth & Preparedness** | Build power, resources, and event readiness over time | S4, S3, S1 | Research, RSS, Full Preparedness, roster quality |
| **Offensive Posture** | Project force and win when we are Invader or in attack events | S3, S2 | Capital Clash (Invader), Alliance Duel, rallies, targeting |
| **Defensive Posture** | Protect assets and win when we are Defender or under pressure | S3, S2, S4 | Capital Clash (Defender), shielding, turrets, durability |

All three are always active; emphasis shifts by event phase and matchup (e.g., more offense in Invader week, more defense in Defender week).

---

# 1. Growth & Preparedness

**Goal:** Increase alliance and member power, resource sustainability, and readiness for Full Preparedness, Alliance Duel, and Cross-State Capital Clash without overspending or burning out.

---

## 1.1 Research Priorities

**Owner: S3 (doctrine) + S4 (resource cost)**  
*LastZ systems: Military Strategies tree, Army Building tree.*

- **Combat multipliers (early ROI)**  
  Prioritize: **Fire Up**, **Armor Upgrade**, **Field Training**, **Camo Coating**. These improve all combat; level evenly where possible so no single branch is a weak link.
- **Rally & coordination**  
  **Joint Operation**, **Mobile Defense**, **Combat Policy**, **Interceptions** — critical for Capital Clash and Alliance Duel. S3 defines minimum levels for rally leads.
- **Unit durability & specials**  
  **Alloy Plate**, **Enhanced Armor**, **Aerocraft Material**; **Demolition Crew**, **Quick Assembly**, **Target Training**, **Recharge Shield**. Balance by troop type usage (e.g., more armor if melee-heavy).
- **Army building foundation**  
  **Mobility Enhancement**, **Melee Combat**, **Weapon Upgrading**, **Stronger Armor**, **Shield Upgrade**, **Tactical Cover** — sustain steady progress; S4 tracks resource cost so members don’t starve Full Preparedness.

**Strategy:** Publish a recommended research order (e.g., “Phase 1: Fire Up/Armor to 5; Phase 2: Joint Operation/Mobile Defense to 5”) and update after major events based on what mattered most.

---

## 1.2 Resource Sustainability

**Owner: S4**  
*LastZ systems: Farming, alliance rules, Full Preparedness tasks.*

- **Farming discipline**  
  Respect alliance farming limits (e.g., 6 attacks per player). S4 publishes the policy; S1 enforces. Avoid over-farming so the alliance stays within rules and members keep RSS for events.
- **Save vs spend windows**  
  Before Capital Clash and Alliance Duel, S4 issues a **Save List** (RSS, speedups, items needed for Full Preparedness and key tasks) and **OK to Use** so members don’t break event readiness ([LastZ guidance](https://lastz.online/svs/): don’t overspend resources needed for Full Preparedness and Alliance Duel).
- **Full Preparedness**  
  Treat Full Preparedness as a primary growth objective. S4 tracks task requirements; S3/S4 advise what to complete in what order to maximize rewards without wasting resources.
- **Long-term RSS**  
  Plan research and upgrades so that members maintain a buffer for healing and speedups during clash weeks.

**Strategy:** Calendar “save windows” (e.g., 3–5 days before Capital Clash weekend) and communicate them clearly so growth doesn’t conflict with event performance.

---

## 1.3 Roster & Readiness

**Owner: S1, supported by S3**  
*LastZ systems: Membership, participation, titles.*

- **Active definition**  
  Define “active” (e.g., login frequency, participation in X events per month). S1 uses this for roster health and readiness reports.
- **Recruitment quality**  
  Recruit for activity and event participation, not just power. S1 aligns standards with S3 (rally fill needs) and S2 (scout needs).
- **Training pipeline**  
  S3 trains new and returning members on: rally join/fill, shielding, event mechanics (Capital Clash, Alliance Duel), and alliance rules. S1 identifies who needs training; S3 delivers.
- **Readiness snapshot**  
  S1 produces a simple readiness product (active count, power band, rally leads/fillers) so S3 can plan operations and S2 can task scouts.

**Strategy:** Growth includes “quality of force”—right size, right activity, right skills. S1 and S3 sync regularly so training and roster management are aligned.

---

## 1.4 Event Readiness Cadence

**Owner: S4 + S3**  
*LastZ systems: Full Preparedness, Alliance Duel, Capital Clash schedule.*

- **Point phase (Sunday–Friday)**  
  States earn points toward State Score; higher score = Invader role (offensive advantage). Growth and daily event participation (Alliance Duel, Full Preparedness, etc.) feed State Score.
- **Pre–Capital Clash checklist**  
  - S4: “Save list” and resource check.  
  - S3: Rally leads assigned; battle drill and turret/capital rules communicated.  
  - S2: Matchup brief (who we face, Defender vs Invader).  
  - S1: Active roster and key contributors confirmed.  
- **Post-event**  
  S4 and S3 note what was short (RSS, speedups, participation); adjust next cycle’s growth and preparedness guidance.

**Strategy:** Growth & Preparedness is the base that makes Offensive and Defensive postures possible. Prioritize Full Preparedness and sustainable resource use so the alliance can fight at full capacity when it matters.

# 2. Offensive Posture

**Goal:** When we are Invader or in attack-focused events, concentrate force, seize objectives (turrets, capital, plunder, rankings), and win decisively.

---

## 2.1 Capital Clash — Invader Role

**Owner: S3 (plans, execution), S2 (targeting)**  
*LastZ systems: Cross-State Capital Clash, turrets, capital, 100% progress, invasion phase.*

- **Objective**  
  Capture the enemy State’s Capital (reach 100% progress) or hold higher progress at time expiry. Turrets support by auto-attacking the enemy capital once captured.
- **Sequence**  
  1. **Turrets first** — Capture the 3 capturable turrets to multiply pressure on the capital and deny the defender easy staging.  
  2. **Capital push** — Concentrate rallies and attacks on the capital; S2 maintains target list and progress tracking.  
  3. **Sustain** — Defend captured turrets (max 10 troops each); replace losses and keep rally tempo so progress doesn’t stall.
- **Timing**  
  Contest phase starts Saturday 12:00; capital contest window 12:00–16:00; event ends Saturday 24:00. S3 publishes a timeline and rally schedule; S2 updates situation at set times (e.g., 12:00, 14:00, 16:00).
- **Rewards**  
  Invader victory: diamond mines (S4 advises on securing and using them); 2 new official alliance positions (S1/leadership plan allocation).

**Strategy:** Mass and tempo. Don’t scatter attacks; use S2 intel to focus rallies on the right objectives at the right time. Rally leads and fillers (from S1 roster) must be pre-assigned so we hit hard from 12:00.

---

## 2.2 Alliance Duel — Offensive Focus

**Owner: S3**  
*LastZ systems: Alliance Duel daily missions, rankings, victory.*

- **Daily objectives**  
  S3 communicates daily rank and mission goals (e.g., Alliance Duel Daily Rank 1, Alliance Duel Victory). Align rallies and attacks with mission requirements.
- **Rally coordination**  
  Designate rally leads and fill requirements; S3 ensures we have enough participants to complete offensive objectives without overextending (S4: avoid burning RSS needed for Full Preparedness or weekend clash).
- **Ranking push**  
  When going for top rank, concentrate activity in the relevant window; S1/S3 ensure key members are online and tasked.

**Strategy:** Offensive posture in Alliance Duel means clear daily targets, coordinated rallies, and disciplined spending so we don’t sacrifice Capital Clash or Full Preparedness.

---

## 2.3 Targeting & Plunder

**Owner: S2 (intel, lists), S3 (execution)**  
*LastZ systems: Enemy state, trucks, HQs, capital zone.*

- **S2 target list**  
  Prioritized list: turrets, capital, high-value HQs, enemy trucks. Update during event; restrict distribution (OPSEC) so enemy can’t anticipate.
- **Plunder objective**  
  “Successfully plunder an enemy State truck” is a personal achievement; S2 identifies opportunities and S3 coordinates or deconflicts so members can complete without wasting rallies.
- **Capital zone**  
  Attacking HQs in the capital zone doubles durability loss for the defender—use when S2 confirms high-value targets and S3 has rally capacity.

**Strategy:** Offense is most effective when S2 tells S3 *what* and *where*; S3 decides *when* and *how* with rallies and battle drill.

---

## 2.4 Offensive Tempo & Economy of Force

**Owner: S3**  
*Principles: Mass, tempo, objective focus.*

- **Concentrate, don’t disperse**  
  Focus rallies on one or two main objectives (e.g., Turret A, then capital) rather than spreading thin.
- **Maintain tempo**  
  Stagger rallies so we sustain pressure; avoid long gaps that let the defender recover.
- **Reserve for decisive moment**  
  Keep a portion of rally capacity and member activity for the critical window (e.g., final push to 100% capital); S3 designates “surge” time and ensures leads/fillers are ready.

**Strategy:** Offensive posture succeeds when we commit enough force at the right place and time. S2 enables that with intel; S3 executes with plans and rally discipline.

# 3. Defensive Posture

**Goal:** When we are Defender or under attack, protect the capital and turrets, minimize losses, and hold the line until time expires or we counter.

---

## 3.1 Capital Clash — Defender Role

**Owner: S3 (plans, execution), S2 (threat picture)**  
*LastZ systems: Defender state, turrets, capital, durability, 10-troop limits.*

- **Objective**  
  Prevent the Invader from reaching 100% capital progress; if time expires, hold higher progress than the enemy. Defend turrets to deny the Invader auto-attacks on our capital.
- **Durability**  
  Attacking HQs *inside* our capital zone costs the attacker 2x durability—S2/S3 can use this to identify where we have a defensive advantage and where the enemy will pay more.
- **Turrets & capital**  
  Max 10 troops per turret, 10 at capital. S3 assigns defenders and backup; S1/S3 ensure key members are available during contest phase (12:00–16:00).
- **Same-state rules**  
  Survivors from the same state don’t fight each other; Alliance A (our state) capturing the capital means Alliance B (same state) won’t attack our capital-held turrets. Coordinate at state level so all state alliances support the same defensive plan.
- **S2 role**  
  Track Invader strength, which turrets are at risk, and capital progress. Feed S3 so we can reinforce threatened points and counter-rally where useful.

**Strategy:** Defense in depth. Hold turrets as long as possible to slow Invader progress; concentrate reinforcements on capital when the enemy pushes. State unity (presidential leadership) is critical so the whole state defends as one.

---

## 3.2 Shielding & Asset Protection

**Owner: S3 (doctrine), S4 (resource advice), members**  
*LastZ systems: Shield, durability, healing.*

- **Shield discipline**  
  When Defender or during high-threat windows, members shield to avoid unnecessary losses. S3 publishes when to shield (e.g., “shield before 12:00 Saturday until contest phase ends”); S4 reminds to keep shield items/speedups for these windows.
- **Durability management**  
  Avoid sending troops into losing battles; S3 trains on when to join rallies vs when to hold. Defending capital zone costs attacker 2x durability—use that to attrit Invader strength over time.
- **Healing**  
  S4 advises on healing capacity and when to heal so we can sustain multiple waves of defense without burning all resources before the event ends.

**Strategy:** Defensive posture preserves force. Shielding and smart durability use keep the alliance able to fight at the end of the event, not just at the start.

---

## 3.3 Zombie Siege & Daily Defense

**Owner: S1 (rules), S3 (mechanics), S4 (farming context)**  
*LastZ systems: Zombie siege, alliance farming rules.*

- **Zombie siege**  
  Follow alliance rules for zombie siege (e.g., defense behavior, when to reinforce). S1 enforces; S3 clarifies tactics so members don’t waste troops or break rules.
- **Farming and defense**  
  Farming limits (e.g., 6 attacks) reduce unnecessary PvP; S4 and S1 keep the alliance in compliance so we don’t attract avoidable retaliation.
- **Rally defense**  
  When defending against rallies (e.g., our HQs or resource nodes), S3 coordinates reinforcements and shield timing; S2 can support with intel on attacker strength if relevant.

**Strategy:** Day-to-day defense is about discipline (shields, rules, durability) so we’re not weakened before major events. S1 and S4 keep the base secure; S3 handles tactical defense when needed.

---

## 3.4 Defensive Depth & Counter-Pressure

**Owner: S3, S2**  
*Principles: Defense in depth, counterattack when prudent.*

- **Layers**  
  Turrets are the outer layer; capital is the inner. Give up turrets only when holding them costs more than the benefit; then concentrate on capital defense.
- **Counter-pressure**  
  When Defender, consider limited counter-rallies (e.g., on Invader turrets or key HQs) if S2 identifies opportunity and S3 has surplus capacity—don’t strip capital defense to do it.
- **State coordination**  
  Defender success often depends on the whole state. Leadership and S3 work with other state alliances so reinforcement and rally defense are coordinated.

**Strategy:** Defensive posture is not passive. We defend priority objectives, preserve strength, and use S2 intel to choose when and where to counterattack without overextending.

# Summary

- **Growth & Preparedness:** Research order, resource sustainability, roster quality, Full Preparedness focus, and event readiness cadence. Build the base so we can fight.
- **Offensive Posture:** Invader execution (turrets then capital), Alliance Duel focus, S2 targeting, and concentrated tempo. Win when we have the initiative.
- **Defensive Posture:** Defender execution (turrets + capital, 10-troop limits), shielding and durability, zombie siege and farming discipline, and selective counter-pressure. Hold when we’re under pressure.

Adjust emphasis by event and matchup; keep S1–S4 coordinated so strategy translates into clear tasks and outcomes.
